LAES Final Project - Aerospace Museum of California VR Experience

project overview

Of the many forms of museum exhibits, virtual reality is a relatively new type of installation, but has quickly become one of the most interesting and engaging experiences museums have to offer. Through virtual reality headsets, kids and adults of all ages can encounter and interact with environments otherwise unknown or inaccessible to them. While the Aerospace Museum of California has a plethora of exhibits ranging from aircraft displays to flight simulators, the museum has yet to install a virtual reality experience.

The Aerospace VR Project is the creation of such an exhibit, where anyone can learn about space and space travel through interactions in a virtual environment. Currently, the experience includes three minigames, custom environments, sound effects, voice lines, and a storyline that guides our player through a series of challenges to repair a damaged space station. The goals of the current iteration of the project are to improve upon the visual, technical, and narrative elements from development teams of past quarters.

images!

Over the past six months, I, alongside the Aerospace VR Senior Project and LAES 301/302 teams, have built and tested versions 1.0 and 2.0 of our Aerospace VR Experience. At the core of our development process were several main ideas, including: an informative experience that reflected real-life space travel, interesting and interactive environments, and universal appeal, particularly to children. As it had been in development in the LAES 301 and 302 classes for several quarters, the most important objective we set for ourselves was the overall improvement of all aspects of the experience.

As Art Lead, I had the task of providing visuals and animations for our game environment, as well as collecting sound effects and helping sequence them within each scene. I used applications such as Canva, Blender, and Pixelorama to create graphics for the monitors in the space shuttle and animations for our AI companion, Houston. Depending on the type of task, I would either work alone or with another person whose skills were needed for the task at hand.
